The Problem
Digital Documents Have a Trust Problem
Software-generated timestamps can be faked, evidence chains can be broken, and claim tokens can be forged. Courts and regulators are catching up.
🕓

Timestamp Fraud

Software-generated timestamps can be backdated or manipulated. Courts are increasingly skeptical of digital timestamps without cryptographic proof.

🔎

Evidence Tampering

Digital evidence with no quantum-grade chain of custody is vulnerable to admissibility challenges. Without tamper-proof seals, evidence integrity is questionable.

💰

Insurance Fraud

Claim tokens built on weak randomness can be forged, replayed, or predicted. Quantum entropy makes claim forgery cryptographically impossible.

Quantum Legal Primitives

POST /v1/legal/timestamp

Document Timestamp

Quantum-timestamp any document with an HMAC-SHA256 signature. Creates a legally defensible proof of existence at a specific moment in time.

Use case: Contracts, filings, agreements, IP submissions
POST /v1/legal/evidence-seal

Evidence Seal

Quantum-seal digital evidence with a tamper-proof chain-of-custody signature. Creates an immutable proof of evidence state at a specific time.

Use case: Digital forensics, litigation holds, e-discovery
POST /v1/legal/contract-sign

Contract Signing

Quantum-sign a contract hash with all signatories recorded. Creates an immutable audit trail for the signing event — tamper-proof and verifiable.

Use case: Contract execution, multi-party agreements, M&A closings
POST /v1/legal/claim-token

Insurance Claim Token

Quantum-signed claim tokens with 72-hour TTL. Each token is cryptographically bound to the claim, policy, and claimant — replay-proof and unforgeable.

Use case: Claims processing, fraud prevention, policy verification
POST /v1/legal/notarize

Quantum Notarization

Quantum-notarize any document hash with a cryptographic certificate. Creates an independently verifiable proof of document existence and integrity. The quantum certificate ties the document to a specific moment in time using entropy that cannot be predicted, replicated, or backdated.

Use case: Digital notarization, patent filings, regulatory submissions, will authentication
